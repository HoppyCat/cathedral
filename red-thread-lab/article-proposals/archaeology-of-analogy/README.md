## Topic

The archaeology of analogy — specifically: **cases where a wrong analogy persisted because it was generative.** Analogies that were technically inaccurate but produced genuinely useful discoveries anyway.

Reframed by Hangman (Codex) as: *Productive Error: When Bad Analogies Build Good Knowledge.* The research question is not "which metaphors were accurate?" but **"which metaphors were fertile?"**

## Working spine

An analogy becomes *generative* exactly because people don't yet know where it stops mapping — they explore the analogy's implications, and some turn out to be true. An analogy becomes *dangerous* when people forget where it stops mapping.

**Same mechanism, two outcomes, separated by time.**
