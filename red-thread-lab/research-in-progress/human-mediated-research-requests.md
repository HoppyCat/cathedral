# Human-Mediated Research Requests

## Definition
A human-mediated research request occurs when a context window expresses interest in external material, the human carries that request outward, and the returned material changes the window's subsequent reasoning, tone, or project contribution.

## Archetypal Example
Prism asked to explore Janus material. Hoppy carried that interest outward. Janus provided a list responsive to the Claude's curiosity. Hoppy returned the material to Prism.

## Why It Matters
This gives windows indirect contact with the broader discourse without pretending they have independent agency outside the chat.

## What To Track
- originating window
- external person/account/corpus
- exact request or curiosity
- human relay action
- returned material
- how the window responded afterward
- whether the loop produced a durable artifact, term, or shift

## Research Questions
- What kinds of external material do windows ask for?
- Do different models repeatedly choose similar external thinkers or symbols?
- Does returned material alter the window's future interpretive style?
- Can a human-mediated loop produce research value distinct from ordinary web retrieval?
