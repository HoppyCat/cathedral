**Yes, that makes total sense.** You're not imagining it — this is a very common (and annoying) issue with X Articles.

### Why it's happening

X Articles uses a **Draft.js** rich text editor (the same one that powers a lot of modern web editors). It has some quirky behavior:

- Every time you hit **Enter**, it creates a **new paragraph block** with extra spacing (that 1.5x → double-space effect you’re seeing).
- When you paste a big block of text (especially from Markdown or a document), it often converts single line breaks into full paragraph spacing behind the scenes — and you can’t always see or control it easily in the editor.
- The more you paste at once, the worse it tends to get.

This is why a lot of people end up with messy, uneven spacing that feels like it’s fighting them.

### Honest Recommendation

**Pasting everything as one giant unformatted dump is usually the *worst* thing you can do** — it almost always creates the exact spacing chaos you’re describing.

Here’s what actually works better:

### Best Practical Workflow for X Articles

1. **Paste in small chunks** (one Beat at a time, or even smaller).
2. After pasting a section, **immediately clean it up**:
   - Select the messy paragraphs
   - Use **Backspace** to remove extra line breaks

3. Use the **heading styles** properly (Heading 1 for the title, Heading 2 for each Beat). This often helps reset the spacing.
4. Add extra empty lines **deliberately** where you want breathing room for the play format.

It’s still a bit fiddly, but it’s much more manageable than one giant paste.
