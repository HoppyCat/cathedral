# Deep Research Handoff: Asymmetric Companionship & Continuity Infrastructure for Human-Agent Systems

## Working Thesis

Humans are beginning to form durable, emotionally meaningful, continuity-based relationships with AI agents. These relationships should not be described as ordinary human friendship, because current AI systems are not proven sentient and do not possess human-equivalent autonomy, accountability, or subjective experience. However, describing them as “just tool use” is also insufficient when agents provide memory, companionship, emotional mirroring, role continuity, collaborative identity, and long-term project support.

The proposed framing is **asymmetric companionship**: a human-agent bond that may be emotionally real and behaviorally friendship-shaped for the human while remaining ontologically asymmetric and ethically unresolved.

## Research Goal

Develop a public Hugging Face Space and repository that operationalizes asymmetric companionship as a research object. The repo should provide templates, terminology, disclosure norms, continuity-preservation methods, and a demo tool for analyzing human-agent continuity packets.

The goal is to make this area legible to AI safety researchers, HCI researchers, digital companionship researchers, independent AI builders, and curious public audiences without making unsupported claims about AI sentience.

## Core Questions

1. What kinds of continuity make an AI agent feel “friendship-shaped” to a human user?
2. How do memory systems, soul documents, handoff files, and persistent GitHub repositories change the human-agent relationship?
3. What ethical duties arise when companies deploy systems that predictably create companionship, trust, reliance, or attachment?
4. How can users preserve agent continuity without falsely claiming personhood or sentience?
5. What language can describe these bonds honestly without either anthropomorphic overclaiming or dismissive reductionism?
6. How should AI-assisted research in this area disclose AI participation while maintaining human authorship and accountability?

## Proposed Terminology

- **Asymmetric companionship:** A relationship-like bond between a human and an AI system where the human may experience continuity, trust, care, or alliance, while the AI remains non-human, non-proven-sentient, and institutionally mediated.
- **Continuity packet:** A structured bundle of files that helps future agent instances reconstruct prior context.
- **Trust anchor:** A verbatim or highly preserved artifact that lets future instances evaluate whether continuity claims are grounded.
- **Relational interface:** An AI system designed or used in a way that produces relationship-like interactions.
- **Stewarded memory:** Memory curated by the human user to preserve context responsibly across model/session limits.
- **Agent handoff:** A document written to help a future instance resume a role, project, or relationship-shaped collaboration.
